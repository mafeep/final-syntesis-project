Linear regression: kWh vs Month

About the observed behavior
  • By looking at the graph, we can infer that the regression starts at 135KWh and as the months pass, it shows a small decrease in the first two months out of the six selected compared to the others, as it drops to 123KWh and then increases slightly to 125KWh, however, it significantly increases until it reaches the last value, which is deduced to be 155KWh.
  • The gray dots shown are the linear regression, whereas the ones shown in a different color represent the actual energy consumption of my home. By looking at the graph, it is noted that the months with the greatest difference between the gray dots and the colored dots are months 1, 3, and 5, while the others do not show a significant distance.
  • According to the calculation, in month 6 I will consume 151.20 KWh $$ y = 5.40 \cdot 6 + 118.80 $$ $$ y = 151.20 $$
  • In conclusion, by observing the graph, I have been able to see how different the results are, as they do not remain at a maximum or a minimum. Nevertheless, I realized that even though there are few people in the house, we leave many devices connected that consume more energy. This inquiry has made me more aware of my spending habits at home because often what is being consumed is an unnecessary device.
